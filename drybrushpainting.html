    DRY BRUSHING

    PACIFIC PAINTING

    Drybrushing Miniatures

    Once the figure has dried and you are happy with the base coat and washing, you come to a technique known as drybrushing.

    In drybrushing, the idea is to highlight the raised surfaces by applying a very small amount of paint to your brush which is a lighter color than the painted area that you're drybrushing.

    Drag the brush across the grain of the texture with very little paint loaded on your brush. This effect will only leave paint on the raised surfaces, simulating how light makes these surfaces lighter in color.

    Applying Technique

    Load your brush with the lighter color that you mixed up. A flat shaped brush usually works best for drybrushing, because it can be easier to control.

    Wipe the brush on your paper towel until very little paint is coming off on the towel. You are now ready to drybrush.

    As with washing, drag the brush across the detail. Don't apply too much pressure or go too slowly, or the bristles of the brush may reach down in the shadowy cracks.

    Hold the brush perpendicular to the figure's highlights as you draw it across it. If you want to get even more outstanding highlights, mix more white into the base color and repeat, this time brushing even lighter over the details. You can do this two or three times, getting lighter each time.

    After drybrushing, be sure to seal the miniature with a matte spray sealant. It is much better to use several fine coats of sealant instead of one heavy one. Even matte sprays will often take on a shine if applied too heavily.
