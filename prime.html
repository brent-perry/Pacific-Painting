    PRIME

    PACIFIC PAINTING

    Apply Primer to Your Miniature

    Here's why this may be the most important step in the entire painting process.

    Why Prime Your Miniatures Before Painting?

    Primer serves two main purposes:

    1. It makes paint stick to the miniature

    2. It provides an undertone for the color applied

    Primer Makes Acrylic Paint Stick

    Acrylic paint doesn't typically adhere well to metal, resin, or plastic, no matter how well you clean your miniature.

    Primer bonds well to metal, giving acrylic paint something to grab hold of.

    Without primer, acrylic paint can flake or chip off easily.

    Less Primer Means Sharper Details

    Applied excessively, primer fills in fine lines and rounds the edges of detail. The best miniatures often have the finest detail.

    Use very thin coats. This helps you to control how much primer you apply. Thin coats also give a slightly rough surface to the miniature.

    So, how do you know when you have applied enough primer? A well cleaned metal mini will typically have a shiny surface, as will many made of plastic or resin. Spray primer in a well-lit area so you can easily see the light reflecting off the portion of the mini you're priming.

    You'll always want to use a flat, or "matte" primer. Spray with short bursts of primer, holding the miniature about a foot or so from the spray can.

    Apply just enough primer so that it eliminates almost all of the reflectivity of the mini, once it dries.
