    PACIFIC PAINTING

    Blending/Layering

    Blending, also known as "layering" is the technique of fading from one color to another with many gradations color or applying successive layers of the same color in thin coats of paint.

    Paint the initial part of the blended area with the initial color. Apply some of the initial color in the area just beside the already painted area, but with a slightly lighter tone.

    Blending allows for more smooth trasitions between colors than can be achieved in other ways. This can even be used in place of the paint-wash-drybrush technique for a lower contrast look. Blending is especially useful when painting large areas. For larger areas, drybrushing often can look unnatural because the contrast is too high on the raised areas.
