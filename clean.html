    PREPARATION

    PACIFIC PAINTING

    Removing Mold Lines

    Plastic or resin miniatures are cast with materials which are much more delicate and easily damaged than metal miniatures. For plastic miniatures, you can typically remove all mold lines with a sharp Exacto knife.

    However, with these softer casting materials, you must use a light touch or you risk cutting notches or removing detail from an otherwise beautiful cast figure.

    In order for casting material to flow into a mold without leaving air pockets, molds often have small openings cut into them to let air and some casting material flow away from the figure itself.

    This leaves little spurs, often found on feet, hands, or other areas where casting material might not otherwise flow. Remove these with an Exacto knife with a motion away from your hands or body. Clean them up with a jeweler's file.

    IMPERFECTIONS

    Re-shaping supplies

    First, you are going to need some items, you will need 2 bowls and a kitchen pot. Bring the pot of water to just before a boil. Then, fill one of the bowls with hot water, and the other with cold water. Then, grab the model that needs correcting.

    Re-shaping steps

    Take your deformed miniature and place the part that needs to be fixed in the boiling water while holding onto the end. Hold the mini in the water for about 3-4 seconds and take it out. DO NOT go past the time or you will lose details in your miniature.

    Once you have taken the miniature out of the boiling water, immediatley move the part that needs to be changed and while holding the desired position, hold the miniature in the cold water for 4-5 seconds.

    Dry the miniature off and repeat the steps as needed. Your miniature should look as good as new.

    RE-SHAPING

    Cleaning

    Before you apply the first dab of color to your miniature, there's a little work to do. Why? Because right out of the package, paint won't stick well to your miniature.

    When metal, plastic, or resin miniatures get cast, casters apply a "release agent" to the mold, an oily or powder substance. This allows the miniature to come out of the mold cleanly.

    The release agent also prevents paint from sticking to the miniature well, so we must remove it.

    Powdered release agents gets into the miniature's cracks, crevices, and fine details. Cleaning the miniature well makes the details really pop out. Until you properly clean a miniature, you don't know what intricate details may remain hidden, so doing this well is really worth the time. Crisping up the edges by cleaning also helps you to find the borders between areas of the miniature, making it easier to paint.
